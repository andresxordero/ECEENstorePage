    ECEEN SAITEX Exhibition Report in Johannesburg, South Africa


    The business show was on last June 2019, it’s around 1 year now, the time runs so fast, but that trip give me a deep impression for South Africa, the social, cultural, city & country…. so that I still want to write something and share to ECEEN favors.

    Introduction of the Show

    ECEEN ELECTRONIC LIMITED participated in SAITEX& SAITEX & SAITEX in the Gallagher Convention Centre, Johannesburg, South Africa from June 23, 2019 to June 25, 2019 & CTW South Africa), We took some of Solar Pack, Solar Charger for Commodity Exhibition.

    The ECEEN team carries a variety of fashionable solar backpacks, smart & fast (QC3.0) solar charger, digital display solar chargers, including the most popular models of ECE-602, ECE-611, ECE -612 in Americans and Europeans, and they have been loved by African friends in the SAITEX show in Joburg too!

    ECEEN took about 30 samples of solar bags, chargers and solar lamps this time. Half of the samples were booked by customers on the first day of the exhibition. By the third day of the exhibition, no samples were available for sale, including our sales staff. The self-used solar backpack was also forcibly bought by guests. We had to buy a bag in local area to hold the accompanying items. The 500 color pages brought were also sent out, African friends love solar products indeed!

    About ECEEN

    ECEEN ELECTRONIC LIMITED was established in 2009, Specializing in the research and development and production of solar bags, solar chargers, solar lights and other related products, they are widely works for smartphones, digital cameras, tablets, laptops and other charging products. The green and environmental of energy-saving, outdoor, and sports products such as solar bags, chargers, and solar lamps etc. are exported to the world and are we received well favors!

    About South Africa

    South Africa is located in the southern hemisphere and has the reputation of "the country of the rainbow". It is located at the southernmost tip of the African continent, with a land area of 1219090 square kilometers. It is surrounded by the Indian Ocean and the Atlantic on the east, south and west. Zimbabwe, Mozambique and Swaziland border. It faces the Indian Ocean and Australia to the east, and Brazil and Argentina to the west.

    South Africa is the second largest economy in Africa, and its citizens have a high standard of living. South Africa has developed financial, legal, communications, energy, and transportation industries. South Africa is a middle-income developing country. South Africa has three capitals. South Africa has a free trade system and is a founding member of the World Trade Organization (WTO). The EU and the United States are South Africa's traditional trading partners, and trade with Asia, the Middle East and other regions is also growing.

    ECEEN’s First Step on South Africa

    Therefore, this time the ECEEN team set foot on the African continent for the first time. First of all, South Africa, South Africa not only has its own economic and trade development, but also radiates the landlocked countries in the southern part of the African continent. Africa has good sunshine, most of the time there is no rain, lack of electricity, ECEEN’s Solar products very suitable on local climate conditions. Many local guests come to find business opportunities, and local Chinese businessmen also come to discuss business.

    The ECEEN sales team also obtained a lot of information from the show, it gave us a lot of ideas, so we developed products suitable for local people, We launched various forms of cooperation with local related companies and teams. This exhibition allows us to look squarely at the African market and see the development potential. It is a successful product promotion show.

    Trips in Johannesburg

    The ECEEN team reserved a few days, visited several companies, and initially reached a cooperation intention; also went to the most famous local malls, Mandela Square, Sandton Center, etc.; also visited Chinatown and felt Strong cost control capabilities from domestic commodities; We also visited the famous Mandela House, Sun City, the Fort Wildlife Park. The endless wildness and grasslands, breathtaking! I also felt the huge gap between local poverty and wealth...
